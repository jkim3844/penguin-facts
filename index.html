Emperor Penguins

These penguins are the largest of all eighteen species of penguins discovered

Signature Characteristics

Adaptation to Cold

Emperor penguins have a unique habitat in the frigid ice of Antartica, where temperatures can drop to below -50°C, and winds can whip up to around 200 km/hr. Their two layers of feathers and fat reserves, as well as their smaller beaks and flippers are all evolutionary adaptations to prevent heat loss.

Breeding

Emperor penguins participate in quick courtship rituals, and incubate eggs during the southern winter months. Females lay only a single egg in May to June, then the male takes over to incubate while the female hunts for the next nine weeks to feed their offspring. Males keep the egg warm in their brood pouch between their feet for 65 to 75 days until the chick hatches.

Swimming Ability

Emperor penguins have some of the highest dives of their avian counterparts, delving as deep as 564 meters and for as long as 32 minutes. Their highly specialized adaptations help them to secure the Antartic silverfish that mainly consists of their diet, as well as other fish species, krill, and squid.
